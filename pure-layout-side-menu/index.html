The O-Town Intro

Traveling to Orlando may seem like an option that's way out of a broke kids mentality of a financial comfort zone. Orlando's fame, as an article from Travel+Lesiure summarizes, O-Town owes its success to the popularity of Disney. Don't be fooled, this city offers more than expensive theme parks and long lines. Orlando is a city where your inner child can break free and the art of explorations exists even if there's a budget to consider. This city is perfect for the three-day vacay that college kids strive for and one they can actually enjoy. There are no shortages of things to do and folks feel the chill vibes that come with this city, it helps that Orlando is also a destination continually on the brink of innovation.

Know Before You Go

Three days can flash by instantly or feel like a lifetime, there's a lot of planning that needs to go into fully enjoying every minute. Know before you go so you can get the most out of every moment. Because Orlando is so popular every day can be crowded but the best way to work around the human cluster is to plan, plan, plan. If you don't know about Orlando the top four topics are important to think about especially when your wallet takes priority. Nothing is free, but there are loopholes to spending the big bucks. Although Disney may have been what put Orlando on the map, there's more than just Disney, Travel+Lesiure points out some of the best shops and restaurants, all that can be located in the downtown area of Orlando. It's home of the business districts and provides a more laid-back scene for those visitors who don't want to be overwhelmed by the Mickey aesthetic.
