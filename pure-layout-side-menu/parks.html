Disney Parks

Park Tips and Tricks

72 Hours leaves plenty of room for those who DO want to splurge a little and get their Disney on. Mark Kahler of Trip Savvy provides incredibly helpful tips for how to visit Orlando on a budget, he breaks it up into segments on times to visit, where to eat, getting around and more. What's important to acknowledge is the thought of theme parks does not have to be scratched off the "To Do List" of places to go because of admission prices. There are factors that can help alleviate the hole that essentially becomes your wallet.

The key things to note: prices are not as high on weekdays as they are on weekends, Florida Residents can take advantage of the many special offers when it comes to purchasing tickets, consider Disney Annual Passes because they cut costs tremendously in the long run, especially for avid Disney lovers. Bring your own food and drinks into theme parks, many people don't think that's allowed, but it is! By doing so, you save time on waiting in line and being able to get the most out of the theme parks you visit and the attractions while saving money too.

In the Know

It all comes down to one's individualistic preferences for the moments they want to experience at a Disney theme park. Either way, be sure to plan accordingly and check out some of the tips on why Disney World Maps can really make a difference when it comes to making the most out of a theme park visit!

One of the best ways to navigate through the theme parks is by downloading the Walt Disney World app. Features such as the directions function allows you to navigate the park easier and more efficiently. You can enhance your experience by finding the wait time function, which allows you to explore the park without wasting time. Walking around endlessly is what creates diversions and takes up time you can be using to enjoy the park to the fullest and make it worth the money you spent to get in. With the features built on the Walt Disney World app you save time, money and planning!

Park Perks

Each Disney park is unique in itself but the perks remain the same: bring your own food there's no limit to what you can bring inside any of the theme parks when it comes to food. Disney may get you at the entry level with admissions prices, but make up for it when it comes to saving your money on food that can be used for something else. Also there are some bonus advancements when it comes to enjoying the theme parks, like meet and greets with some of Disney's most iconic characters. It doesn't cost any additional fees for you to re-create some of your best childhood memories.
